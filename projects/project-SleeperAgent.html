Sleeper Agent

A solo stealth-action prototype in Unreal Engine 5 — from GDD to playable implementation, featuring procedural hit reactions as a technical highlight.

YouTube GDD

Overview

Sleeper Agent is a solo stealth-action project inspired by Splinter Cell. It goes from a complete Game Design Document through to a playable UE5 prototype. The video playlist shows gameplay extracts from the prototype, covering the various systems explored during development.

Core Systems

  • Stealth mechanics — visibility cones, noise propagation, and cover interactions
  • AI awareness states and patrol behavior design
  • Mission structure with primary and optional objectives
  • Procedural hit reactions — direction and impact weight calculated in real-time using UE5 technology, driving contextual reaction animations

Technical Notes

The standout technical feature is the procedural hit reaction system: using newer UE5 capabilities, the direction and "heaviness" of each hit are computed at runtime to generate contextually appropriate reactions, rather than playing a fixed animation. The full GDD documenting all systems and design decisions is available to download above.
